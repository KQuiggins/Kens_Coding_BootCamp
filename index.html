Frequently Asked Questions

Full stack development refers to web development taking place in both the front and back ends of a website. In other words, the process may involve a combination of underlying database work, user-facing website construction, and client-focused communication aimed at the planning and maintenance of projects (among many other responsibilities).

A front-end developer mainly codes what is visible to the end user, mainly in web or mobile apps. They are responsible for the layout or schema for these type of applications.

Back-end developers are the experts who build and maintain the mechanisms that process data and perform actions on websites. Unlike front-end developers, who control everything you can see on a website, back-end developers are involved in data storage, security, and other server-side functions that you cannot see.
